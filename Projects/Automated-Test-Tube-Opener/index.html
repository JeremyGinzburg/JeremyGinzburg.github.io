Client-Sponsored Engineering Design

Designed and built a proof-of-concept laboratory automation device for Duke University's David Lab capable of simultaneously removing eight test tube caps. The system reduces repetitive strain, minimizes cross-contamination, and improves laboratory efficiency through automated mechanical actuation.

CAD Rack & Pinion Arduino Client Project

Project Overview

The Problem

Researchers in Duke University's David Lab manually opened and reattached hundreds of test tube caps during routine sample preparation.

This resulted in:

  • Repetitive wrist and forearm fatigue
  • Greater risk of cross-contamination from placing caps on the bench
  • Over 1 hour to process just 20 test tubes

Design Requirements

  • Open 8 laboratory tubes simultaneously
  • Reduce processing time by at least 50%
  • Hold caps after removal to prevent contamination
  • Operate hands-free after cycle initiation
  • Fit within a 12" × 16" laboratory footprint
  • Provide safe, repeatable operation

Mechanical Design

The automated tube opener was designed as a modular electromechanical system composed of independently developed mechanisms. Each subsystem was prototyped, tested, and refined before integration into the final eight-tube automation platform.

Automation System

An Arduino Uno coordinates nine DC motors to automate the tube uncapping and recapping process. Eight motors independently rotate the cap grippers, while a ninth motor drives the rack-and-pinion transport mechanism. A single push button initiates each stage of the workflow, allowing users to process samples with minimal manual interaction.

Controller

  • Arduino Uno microcontroller
  • Hard-coded motor rotations

Motor System

  • 9 DC motors

  • 1 rack drive motor

User Interface

Power

Testing & Validation

The completed prototype was evaluated through repeated functional testing to verify that the mechanical and automation systems satisfied the primary project requirements. Individual subsystems were tested during development, followed by more than twenty full-system demonstrations.
