EMG-Controlled Prosthetic Hand

(In Progress)

Designing a tendon-driven prosthetic hand that will ultimately use forearm EMG signals to control grasping motions in real time.

Project Overview

This project focuses on designing and prototyping a low-cost, tendon-driven prosthetic hand using 3D printed parts, embedded electronics, and signal processing. The long-term goal is to convert forearm EMG signals into coordinated finger motion for intuitive prosthetic control.

Design Objectives

  • Affordable 3D-printed prosthetic hand
  • Tendon-driven finger actuation
  • Arduino-based control electronics
  • EMG signal integration
  • Coordinated grasping motion

System Architecture

The current prototype validates the mechanical actuation system before integrating EMG signal processing. Four independently actuated fingers are driven by SG90 servo motors through tendon routing while an Arduino Uno provides synchronized control. Future iterations will replace pre-programmed servo commands with real-time forearm EMG input.

Mechanical Design

The prosthetic hand was designed entirely in SolidWorks with a focus on modularity, low-cost manufacturing, and tendon-driven actuation. Multiple design iterations improved joint motion, tendon routing, and overall assembly before integration into the complete four-finger prototype.

Tendon Actuation

Fishing line tendons driven by independent SG90 servo motors generate finger flexion while simplifying the actuation system.

Elastic Extension

Rubber bands provide passive finger extension, allowing the fingers to reopen without additional actuators.

Pin Joints

M3 screw pins create durable rotational joints that are simple to assemble, service, and redesign.

Modular Design

Individual fingers can be redesigned independently and SG90 servos are currently attached to a temporary mounting system.

Engineering Development Log

The prototype has been developed through iterative CAD design, rapid prototyping, electronics integration, and mechanical testing. Each stage introduced new design challenges that guided the next iteration of the system.

June 25, 2026

Concept Research

Investigated existing prosthetic hand mechanisms, tendon-driven actuation, and wrist designs to establish the overall architecture for the project.

June 26 – June 29

First Finger Prototype

Designed the fingertip, middle phalanx, and base in SolidWorks. Printed multiple iterations to improve joint alignment, screw tolerances, and tendon routing.

July 1 – July 3

Tendon Mechanism

Replaced the original tendon routing, increased hole sizes, integrated elastic bands, and achieved the first fully functional tendon-driven finger.

July 4 – July 9

Servo Integration

Transitioned from manual tendon actuation to SG90 servos using an Arduino Uno. Developed servo mounting hardware and custom firmware for adjustable finger motion.

July 10

Four-Servo Electronics

Designed the complete four-servo wiring architecture, validated the circuit in TinkerCAD, and prepared the hardware using an external regulated power supply.

Current

Four-Finger Prototype

Building and testing the complete four-finger mechanism while calibrating tendon lengths, servo angles, and mechanical alignment prior to palm development and EMG integration.
